Anonymous 24-Jul-20 12:29 PM
@Anonymous Lanes the lectures did not cover density operations. For example, you cannot represent the subsystems of an entangled state as a unitary vector. Also when errors are there, the state is error free with probability (1-p) and erroneous with probability p. These are like classical mixtures, and cannot be readily represented as a unit vector in Hilbert space. Therefore, visualization as a Bloch Sphere becomes necessary there. As for the states we are looking into primarily, called pure states, can be efficiently viewed as vectors. But Block Sphere provides a more complete generalized visualization.
