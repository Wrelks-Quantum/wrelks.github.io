Not to just download archives, there are many many paper on transconductance or electron transfer across molecular junctions. My interest is in DFT for LUMO/HOMO gaps maximization using QC for our lab. I post two papers I am reading which are not peer reviewed and come from ChemRxiv.org. "Charge-Transfer Selectivity and Quantum Interference in Real-time Electron Dynamics: Gaining Insights from Time-Dependent Configurarion Interaction Simulation", R. Ramskrishnan, March 9, 2020, is too big 10.5MB to post here. Visit: https://chemrxiv.org/articles/preprint/Quantum_Interference_in_Real-Time_Electron-Dynamics_Gaining_Insights_from_Time-Dependent_Configuration_Interaction_Simulations/11086292 for details. For me this is an interesting read. Another preprint: "Fine-tuning the Optoelectronic Properties of Borophene by Strain", L. Adamska, S.Sharifzadeh, 2017 is closer to my interests in physics and materials as it pertains to Boron nanotubes instead of Carbon. Although dated this paper refers to the areas related to the above paper on optoelectric properties. Squids are theorectical devices for molecular optoelectronics, of interest to some. Again my research is theoretical computer science regarding super-fast algorithms for computational methods in the computational chemistry domain. I seek qc analogues to DFT and other algorithms.
