Frequently Asked Questions What is the difference between the orange names and yellow names?  Name colors represent roles that various people have. All students are yellow, and orange names are students participating in both the labs and lectures. (NOTE: It may take a bit for your role to be updated - please give admins some time to update). How do I know if I am in the labs? - You received an email confirmation saying you were in the labs - (Optional) You confirmed lab participation with a checkbox form sent in the confirmation - You received your invitation to join Discord on July 18 Will the lectures be recorded? What about the labs?  Yes! You can join live, or watch the content on-demand. Links will be posted daily in Discord.  Can I submit labwork after the solution is posted the following day?  Yep! The deadline for all final lab work is 11:59 EDT on July 31st.  Everyone is encouraged to work on those with study groups (or on your own if you prefer) and when the solutions are provided (if desired) you can review the solution, and work on implementing with those additional insights and instructions, and resubmit your notebook. The purpose of the "homework" isn't for grading assessment so much as empowering everyone to gain applied understanding and becoming comfortable with and about Quantum computing. Do i need to download anything to participate?  Nope! Everything can be done in-browser.  Tip for all! Manage your notifications - you can right click on the server as a whole or individual categories and channels - and set your notification from "All messages" to "Only @ mentions". It will help a lot! Don't forget to check out the Attendee Guide - it has answers to some of the questions that haven't even been thought of yet!

Joining Study groups: Ignore the !studygroup and ;group commands; they don't do anything useful. If you are a lab student, you already have access to all of the lab groups! Check them out under the Study Group Section. You don't need to "join" any channels. If you want to be part of a study group, interact with people! Say hi, share a little bit about yourself, or strike up a conversation. The harder part is "leaving" channels. You can mute a channel, which will disable notifications and hide the unread indicator. This works well in conjunction with the Hide Muted Channels option in the server settings. Unfortunately, you have to mute each channel individually, unless you want to mute all study groups by muting the Study Group category.

On Certificates for Lab Students as posted here by @Anonymous (thanks!) mentioned by Abe in one post: Yes, [certificates will be granted] for lab students who score a 65% or above on the labs. Cumulative score simply means how many of the labs you got correct. So far there have been 5 lab exercises. By the end, you will have gone through 9, and so 65% means getting >5 of them right. On one of the upcoming days, you will have two exercises in the same lab, so each one will account for 0.5 of that lab. I hope that clarifies things :) So just to quickly clarify -- cumulative simply means the sum of all your correct exercise submissions. There's no partial grading of each exercise that you need to worry about. (edited)

Copied from @Anonymous here: https://discord.com/channels/729706617951485954/735115703346266172/738662815518162984 How to Run Labs from Google Colab Step 1. Unzip Lab Folder Step 2. Open up lab folder, and you'll see ex1.ipynb, a grading_tools folder and a resources folder. Step 3. Zip up grading_tools and zip up resources folders. You should now have a grading_tools.zip and resources.zip in your directory. Step 4. Upload ex1.ipynb to your Google Drive Step 5. Click on ex1.ipynb on your Google Drive and click "Open With" and select Google Colab. You should now see your lab notebook Step 6. Go to the left toolbar and select the Folder icon. The notebook is still setting up a runtime, so wait a few seconds until you are connected and see a folder called sample_data. This means you are now connected to a runtime. Step 7. Drag the two zip files you created from your computer into the file area. You should see grading_tools.zip and resources.zip loaded into your Colab directory Step 8. Before you run any code, we need to unzip those two folders. Add a code block and add the following lines
!unzip grading_tools.zip !unzip resources.zip
Step 9. Run that block of code, and that will unzip those two folders in your Google Colab notebook. You are now ready to start working on the lab :) Note that when the runtime expires or whenever you reload the notebook, you will need to upload those two zip files again and run the code that unzips those two folders Thanks to @Anonymous for this tutorial!
