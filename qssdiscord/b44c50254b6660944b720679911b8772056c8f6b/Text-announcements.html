We now have a MathBot that will help you use LaTeX to convert your math text to images and make reading notations more comprehensible! Please note that this command is limited to relevant channels like discussion, sandbox and study groups, and will NOT work in off-topic channels like support or general. Below are a few commands we recommend you try out: =tex - Render LaTeX as an image. =wolf - Query Wolfram|Alpha. =pup - Query Wolfram|Alpha for refined results. =calc - Powerful and fast calculator. =oeis - Query the Online Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences =help - The bot sends you a DM with information on how to use the bot. Anonymous 28-Jul-20 07:24 PM
FINAL PROJECT ANNOUNCEMENT: How Low Can You Go? We at IBM know how low we can get, but how will you fare? With the learnings from the final days of class focused on Quantum Chemistry, this Final Project is applying those subjects with a hands-on exercise. You will be simulating the ground state of the molecule Lithium Hydride (LiH) with the lowest energy and error. Your Final Project is to complete the molecular simulation in the fewest number of steps, and the highest level of chemical accuracy.  The Final Project is optional, you will need to opt-in to the final project. On Wednesday, August 5th at 10:00 AM EDT, we will kick off with Random Team Generation (this is the suggested way to approach the final project - in a small group). We'll use your location, time zone, and other information provided to form these groups. Every team will be made up of 5 people, paired up based on our own algorithm using Qiskit (note from justjosie: yes, I understand this sounds corny, but Quantum Computing CAN be used for the more mundane everyday things too!). All teams will receive a Jupyter Notebook containing the full details of the final project challenge waiting for you. After some introductions and learning about each other's strengths, dive in to the challenge! The project will be due on August 7 at 10:00am EDT.  Your final output will be in the form of a graph, which we will add to a showcase. Once the project concludes, you will be able to see how your results compare to other teams, and to the IBM Quantum team's results. Who will go the lowest? Will anyone optimize beyond what we at IBM Quantum has been able to do?
About the Challenge In the Final Project we will design, implement, and simulate a VQE algorithm for calculating the ground state energy of the LiH molecule. We will explore different choices of designing VQE algorithms and compare their performance under specified assumptions to each other and to the classically computed exact solution. In addition, we will look at how realistic device specific noises could impact the performance of VQE algorithms.  When designing an algorithm to be run on an actual quantum computer, there are a lot of variables to consider and the choices you make for the algorithm could dynamically change given different hardware specifications. Therefore there is no right answer for how you should design your algorithm. The goal of this project is for you to understand the different components that comprise a VQE algorithm and showcase your understanding by comparing the performance of different algorithm instances. IMPORTANTLY: Each team will only be able to submit their completed project once! You want to make sure to work together to make it count! Next Steps Tomorrow afternoon, there will be a sign up form for the final project posted right here in Discord. If you are interested in participating in the final project, please make sure and fill out the sign up form with as much information as you can! Teams will be identified as part of the morning kick off of the final project - the deadline for signups will be announced tomorrow.
